Stage1: Eliciting Characteristics of Affective Engagement

Methods

In stage 1, we examined the role and characteristics of AE in evaluating visualizations through an exploratory qualitative study, from which 11 indicators of AE were proposed. To elicit characteristics of AE in InfoVis, we conducted a lab study using a mixed-methods (i.e., quantitative and qualitative) approach. We had two primary research questions for the study: (1) What are the characteristics of affective engagement in an InfoVis context? and (2) What factors contribute to the development and fluctuation of subjective engagement over time as users interact with visualizations?

11 Indicators

Levels Indicators Description
Behavior (user's specifc behavioral pattern) Fluidity The flow of use is continuous, smooth, not disrupted
Enthusiasm The user is interested, eager, proactively involved, and motivated
Curiosity The user is inquisitive or investigative
Discovery The user discovers something new or noticeable
Judgement (user's judgment of the visualization) Clarity The visualization express a clear concept or message; the story is clear or can be easily interpreted
Storytelling The visualization tells a compelling story or has a persuasive narrative style
Creativity The visualization helps users generate or express creative and innovative thoughts or ideas
Feeling (user's reaction of the visualization) Entertainment The user feels the visualization is fun, interesting, or charming
Untroubling The user feels content and doesn't feel upset or frustrated
Captivation The user is concentrating, absorded
Pleasing The user is impressed by the visualization (e.g., visually, concept-wise)
