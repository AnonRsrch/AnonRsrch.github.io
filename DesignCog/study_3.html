Study 3: Generative Abstractions in Academic Literature

Methods

To study this research--practice gap, we conducted another literature search to determine if it is plausible that academic researchers are in fact employing generative abstractions in their own design work, yet are not explicitly building them into design frameworks.

Initial Codebook

We coded for IS/CI(s) in the areas of the papers that discussed visualization tools or techniques, as identified by our regular expression searches. We compiled excerpt(s) that described or specified IS/CI, the visual representation or visualization technique being utilized for that IS/CI, and screenshots and visual encodings of those visual representations

Codes for Information Strcuture

10 preset codes derived from C30, which proposed fundamental structures for organizing information that can be used as conceptual tools: branch, cell/container, cycle, link/connection, hierarchy, list/sequence, stack, track, area/spatial/bounded region, and token/entity/data point.

Information Strcuture Definition
IS1: Branch Map information items onto visualizations and organize them in the representation space in a branched and/or subdivided fashion. (p. 64)
IS2: Cell/container Map information items onto visualizations and organize them by segmenting, compartmentalizing, or containing them within cell-like structures. (p. 63)
IS3: Cycle Map information items onto visualizations and organize them in a circular, wheel-like, rotational, spiral, and/or cyclical fashion. Using a circular structure in a visualization does not necessarily mean that it is based on the Cycle pattern (p. 64)
IS4: Link/connection Map information items onto visualizations and organize them by connecting them together using paths, routes, lines, or other similar structures. (p. 64)
IS5: Hierarchy Map information items onto visualizations and organize them in a hierarchical, multi-level, pyramid-like fashion, where higher levels are superior to or contain and encompass lower level items. (p. 64)
IS6: List/sequence Map information items onto visualizations and organize them by placing in a sequential, successive fashion. (p. 64)
IS7: Stack Map information items onto visualizations and organize them by placing on top of one another in a piled or stacked fashion. Visual representations are often placed on top of one another such that they are touching or are very close together (p. 64)
IS8: Track Map information items onto visualizations and organize them in a lane-, stripe-, and/or track-like fashion. (p. 63)
IS9: Area/spatial/bounded region Map information items onto visualizations in such a way that their boundary, shape, region, and/or area are encoded. (p. 63)
IS10: Token/entity/data point Map one or more information items onto a visualization that can be regarded as a unit, whether in atomic form or composite form made of discrete parts. Most used pattern for representing information. Most visualizations are based on a blending of Token with other patterns. Instantiations are often small. (p. 63)
