Study 3: Generative Abstractions in Academic Literature

Methods

To study this research--practice gap, we conducted another literature search to determine if it is plausible that academic researchers are in fact employing generative abstractions in their own design work, yet are not explicitly building them into design frameworks.

Initial Codebook

We coded for IS/CI(s) in the areas of the papers that discussed visualization tools or techniques, as identified by our regular expression searches. We compiled excerpt(s) that described or specified IS/CI, the visual representation or visualization technique being utilized for that IS/CI, and screenshots and visual encodings of those visual representations

Frequency of (a) Information structure (IS) and (b) Communicative Intent (CI) from study 3, ordering by the number of counts. CI with less than 5 counts are not listed in this figure.

Common visualization Technique

A smaller subset including only the papers from the ACM SIGCHI conference (2016-2018) were used to further-analyze how authors of visualization techniques or systems reported on their intentions during the design process.
