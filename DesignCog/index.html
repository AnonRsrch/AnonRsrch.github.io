Conceptual Supports in InfoVis Design: Information Structures and Communicative Intents as Generative Abstractions

Abstract

Visualization designers require a range of supports---including frameworks, heuristics, and guidelines---to successfully navigate design problems. While numerous design supports have been proposed, few have focused on how designers' conceptualization can be supported while mapping abstract information onto visual forms. In this paper, we present three studies related to this issue. First, we compiled existing classifications in the InfoVis literature to determine the kinds of support being provided. Second, we investigated online practitioner-oriented resources for visualization design to see what practitioners were actually using. Third, we analyzed InfoVis literature to determine how researchers conceptualize aspects of their design process. Our findings uncover two types of conceptual support that serve generative functions in InfoVis design: information structures (IS) and communicative intents (CI). We report our results, discuss implications for visualization design, and point toward future work in this area.
